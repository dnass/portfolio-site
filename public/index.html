The CDC Says Gun Injuries Are on the Rise. But There Are Big Problems With Its Data.

The Trace / FiveThirtyEight • October 2018 • Words and data

Sean Campbell and I teamed up with FiveThirtyEight‘s quantitative editor Mai Nguyen to investigate the flawed approach used by the CDC to generate estimates of nonfatal firearm injuries. We developed a methodology for comparing the trends indicated by the CDC‘s data with those in other public health datasets, and found that the high degree of uncertainty around the CDC‘s estimates had serious implications for public health research in the United States. The article was also published on FiveThirtyEight.

What Members of Congress Do — and Don’t — Say After Mass Shootings

The Trace • February 2018 • Words and interactive

After the Parkland shooting, I wanted to find out how prevalent the widely mocked phrase “thoughts and prayers” actually was. So I used ProPublica‘s Congress API to find the text of more than 800 statements released by members of Congress in the aftermath of mass shootings. My analysis found that two-thirds of all the statements contain the word “pray” – and also that Marco Rubio is the only Republican ever to utter the words “gun control” after a mass shooting. As the final step of the interactive, a search interface lets users query the data themselves.

The ATF Received 36,000 Comments on Bump Stocks. They’re Overwhelmingly Anti-Regulation.

The Trace • February 2018 • Words and graphics

The public comment period for a proposed ATF regulation to regulate bump stocks as machine guns – effectively a ban – drew more than 36,000 comments. My colleague Sean Campbell and I scraped the text of these comments and, using a combination of Python scripts and hand-tagging, calculated the share of comments for and against the regulation, as well as the percent that were unique or form letters. We found that the responses were overwhelmingly opposed to the regulation, backing up previous research which found that gun rights supporters are more politically engaged than their opponents.

Stolen Guns: Rising Firearm Theft Is Quietly Fueling Violent Crime

The Trace / NBC • November 2017 • Interactive

The Trace‘s Brian Freskos launched an enormous investigation into gun theft by requesting data on stolen and recovered guns from hundreds of law enforcement agencies around the country. I helped to clean and analyze that data, writing scripts to normalize hundreds of spelling variations, identify errors in serial numbers, and comb through the records to match stolen and recovered firearms. We partnered with more than a dozen NBC stations, who used the data, as well as graphics I produced, to report on gun theft in cities around the country. We also published the raw data. This project was awarded Best Reporting by Independent Digital Media at the 2018 Deadline Club Awards.

History of crimes, ineptitude for Cambodia‘s flag of convenience scheme

The Phnom Penh Post • September 2016 • Words and graphics

Until 2016, Cambodia ran a flag of convenience program, under which foreign nationals could register their ships to fly the Cambodian flag. To investigate the program‘s shady history, I scraped GPS tracking data for hundreds of Cambodian-flagged vessels, which reporter Jack Davies and I combed through to identify ships that had called at ports in North Korea and Crimea. I also designed the article‘s maps.

Only 20 percent of PM’s recent Facebook ‘likes’ from Cambodia

The Phnom Penh Post • March 2016 • Words and graphics

In 2016, Cambodian Prime Minister Hun Sen was engaged in a Facebook popularity war with then-opposition leader Sam Rainsy. Using data from a social media analytics site, I discovered that hundreds of thousands of the Prime Minister‘s Facebook likes had come from countries outside of Cambodia, such as India and the Philippines. The story sparked international coverage and led to a legal dispute that between Sam Rainsy and Hun Sen that is still ongoing. This article was also published in Khmer.
